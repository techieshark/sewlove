#whatwarmsmywinter space activation

Auckland Council enlisted the Sew Love community to knit and yarn bomb Bledisloe Pocket Park in the midst of grey winter. Every lunchtime we had tea, music, knitting lessons and people from all walks of life gathered to add more rainbows to the cement of the city centre.

Sew Some Love at Splore (Feb 2014)

In a 6x3m gazebo we housed a costume making and craft zone for splore goers. Workshops included: Gods Eye mobiles, Tent Bunting, Fabric Flower Crowns, & postcards. 100% recycled materials, and leaving no trace behind.

Sewcials in St Kevins

Monthly on a friday night we hosted a sewcial (say it like social) where sewers could bring their own projects and join in a mending, knitting, stitching evening with new friends, wine & nibbles. We could pop this up at your place too, get in touch and gather your pals.

Mancraft Moments

Sewing is not just for the ladies, at Sew Love in St Kevins, and at all of our pop ups, we have been stoked to see so many guys giving making a go. Mending ripped jeans over and over, handstitching leather wallets, document holders for outdoor education, aprons for food trucks, the lot! Follow on for more at #mancraftmoments

Fashion Revolution Day

April 24 marks the day of the Rana Plaza Factory Collapse in Bangladesh, and a reminder to ask WHO made your clothes? and in WHAT conditions? We hosted a photobooth in the shop window for our community of customers and sewists who know #imademyclothes!

What we do.

Sew Love is all about providing an opportunity, space and resources for people and communities to come together, do something practical for themselves, each other and the environment. In our workshops, we focus on re-using discarded resources and treasures, meeting your neighbours, and creating a more meaningful life.

Now touring the North Island.

We are taking the fun of creative sustainability on the road this summer in a mobile sewing lounge/tea house/op shop loving super truck! We will be parking up beachside or at your local op shop with treasures and shared resources so you can have a go at sewing, mending, sipping iced tea, and keeping our Aotearoa clean and beautiful.

Sitka have jumped on board this summer tour and we will be selling Sitka seconds and doing FREE Sitka repairs everywhere we go, to help share their ethos of community & sustainability!

If you see us parked up, come and say kia ora!
